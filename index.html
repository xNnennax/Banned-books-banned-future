Introduction

There are many diferent social issues in the world lke racism , seism , unemployment etc. There is one that is not talked about enough and this is the topic of book bans. Book bans are when a certain book is banned because they are deemed to have too much inappropriate content such as racism, sexism, sexually explicit, offensive language, etc. Since this is the case, some parents are not ready to expose their kids to content like this.

Why is it important books don't get banned?

Banned books might seem like they ae awful but they aren't. Banned books actually teach important life lessons one might not get in school or anywhere else. These lessons are very important because they tell life as it is .

Facts on Book bans

More than 2500 books were banned in 138 school districts in 32 states in the 2021-22 school year. Books dealing with sexual themes about a straight white protagonist haven't been challenged . 2532 books were banned which affected 1648 unique book titles. Since July 2022, 139 additional book bans have been made . This was tracked by PEN America. 41% of banned books from the time of july 2021- june 2022 were about lgbtq+ characters 40% of books/stories of people of color have also been banned. “The hate you give” was banned in 17 districts. “Gender queer” by Maia Kobabe was banned in 41 school districts. The first book ban in the U.S. took place 1637. The #The 1 banned book in the world is “The catcher in the rye” by J.D. Selinger. Books can be banned if they are deemed inappropriate , sexually explicit , violent , racism, etc. In the last school year, more than 1600 books have been banned. Banning books are said to restrict information. It is said that banning books discourages freedom of thought.
